There are N customers in a restaurant, each numbered from 1 to N. A sushi chef carries out Q operations for customers. In each operation, the chef selects the customer with the minimum number of sushi dishes eaten among the first a_i customers. If there are multiple customers with the same minimum, the one with the smallest number is chosen. The chef puts a dish of sushi on the selected seat, and this customer eats it. This process is repeated b_i times. The goal is to calculate how many dishes of sushi each customer has eaten by the end.
